Cold Brew

What Is Cold Brewing

Cold brew is a coffee brewing method that uses cold or room-temperature water to steep coarsely ground coffee over an extended period, typically 12 to 24 hours. This slow extraction produces a smooth, well-rounded coffee with low acidity and a naturally mellow taste.

Variety of Brewing Methods

Unlike traditional hot brewing methods, cold brew emphasizes sweetness and clarity while reducing bitterness. The result is a refreshing and versatile beverage that can be served over ice, diluted with water or milk, and enjoyed in a variety of coffee-based drinks.
