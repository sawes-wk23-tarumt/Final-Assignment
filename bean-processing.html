Bean Processing

What Is Bean Processing

Bean processing is a method used to remove the coffee fruit from the bean after harvesting. This step is essential because it greatly influences the coffeeâ€™s flavor, aroma, and body. Different processing techniques highlight various characteristics in the bean, shaping how the coffee tastes in the final cup.

Variety of Bean Processing

There are several bean processing methods, such as washed, natural, and honey. Each method affects sweetness, acidity, and texture in unique ways. Understanding these processes helps coffee drinkers appreciate why coffees from the same origin can taste remarkably different.
